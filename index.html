About Us

Holistic Education Advocating Leadership (HEAL) is a 501(c) 3 non-profit initiative, tackling the epidemic of rape and sexual assault in Liberia, West Africa and communities alike. Among other things, HEAL wishes to raise awareness about the alarming high number of rape against children under the age of twelve, educate Liberians and communities alike on the long and short term effects of rape and sexual assault, advocate for victims of rape and sexual assault, and make DNA forensics available in order to provide rape kits, and to seek justice for rape victims. HEAL ultimately wants to open a forensics laboratory in Liberia with the sole purpose of providing DNA analysis that will help bring rape perpetrators to justice.

Projects

Safe Touch, Unsafe Touch

“Safe Touch, Unsafe Touch” is a culturally sensitive workshop designed by HEAL and the First Witness Child Advocacy Program of Minnesota. This workshop teaches children the signs of people with pedophiliac interest versus those that are being friendly. It teaches parents and children what to do should they become a victim of rape and/or sexual assault. It teaches parents ways to recognize signs of both types of abuse and teaches therapeutic ways to communicate with children. The workshop also emphasizes the long term and short term effects of rape and sexual assault. It incorporates the appropriate actions needed in order to preserve DNA forensics in rape cases and how parents can seek resources to actively be apart of advocating for their children. This workshop is to be conducted at schools throughout Liberia, at community events within Liberia and within the Liberian community here in the USA.

Training

One of the primary focus of HEAL partner, First Witness Advocacy of Duluth is advocating for children. Among other things, HEAL founder, MawataKamara and Operations Director, Katherine Redd were both trained by First Witness on the principles of child advocacy and how the model used by First Advocacy center can be applied in Liberia and communities like. HEAL will take members of FIrst Witness for a week of training of professions in Liberia and provide means of continuing education on how be a child’s advocate above all else. In addition, HEAL is planning to partner with Sorenson Forensics to host a training in Liberia on how to handle evidence involving a rape case as well as how to use DNA results to prosecute rapists.

My Childhood is your future

Traditionally in Liberia and countries alike, there is an unspoken rule that children are meant to be seen and not heard. In fact, if a child is to speak without permission or speak while adults are conversing, that child is often label as disrespectful. Although this mentality has its significance within Liberian cultural, it is often misused and exaggerated. This mentality is also often the underlying cause of unjust silence amongst children; leaving them more vulnerable to crimes such as rape and violence. On Friday, October 11, 2013, HEAL hosted its first annual peaceful march in order to encourage children to be their own advocates and remind them of the power of their voice. It also while gave children an opportunity to celebrate their childhood and have fun. The Day was dedicated to the children of Liberia. It was a chance for them to express themselves, be heard and PLAY! The day started with a march from B.W Harris to ATS stadium and ended with food, music and fun activities at the stadium.

Small Small Thing Documentary

HEAL has been fortunate to have had the opportunity to partner with the creators of the documentary, Small Small Thing. The award winning documentary tells the story of the rape and death of a nine years old girl, Olivia Zinnah. The documentary also does an excellent job of highlighting many social and judicial issues surrounding rape and sexual assault in Liberia. HEAL and the creators of the documentary wish to travel across the United States and globally shine the light on the raping of children in Liberia.

Palava H.U.T.

The Palava HUT is an open forum to be conducted in various location within the community. This forum involves a discussion amongst the community members about rape and sexual assault and the effects it has on the community. The acronym H.U.T stands for Hearing, Understanding and Teaching. The goal of the Palava HUT is to allow community members to be informed, discuss and provide feedback, create teachers within our communities  and remind adults that they are children’s biggest advocate.

Proof is in the DNA

HEAL has partnered with Tru-Vision Clothing,  to create a t-shirt champaign. Each t-shirt, has the slogan “the proof is in the DNA”. The t-shirts were designed through a joint effort by HEAL and Tru-Vision Clothing. The campaign includes images (shown left)  from known members of the Liberian community stating how they feel about rape and sexual assault in Liberia, and within the Liberian community or why they support the causes of HEAL.
